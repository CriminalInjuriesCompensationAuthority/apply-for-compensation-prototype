Introduction to Private Beta New online service for non-complex sexual assault applications

This guidance is for answering calls from users of our new Private Beta application service.

About Private Beta

Private Beta is the project phase where we build a working version of our new service. We then test the service with real users.

We will invite users into Private Beta in one of three ways:

  • Outside organisations, for example Victim Support, will give users the service link
  • CSC staff will send users the link
  • CSC staff will complete usersâ€™ applications for them

You may also get calls from users who have already started their applications but call us for help.

First version

The first version of the service will serve users who:

  • have an email address
  • are British citizens or EU nationals
  • are 18 or over
  • do not have a representative
  • are victims of sexual assault or abuse
  • opt for us to make a decision on police evidence only

Key changes

Compared to our current online application service, users will:

  • not have to create an account
  • not be able to save and return to the application later
  • not receive an application summary for a telephone application
  • answer less questions. The user journey is 18 to 27 questions, rather than 41 to 64

Guidance updates

We will update the guidance if we:

  • identify new issues
  • improve and refine the design
  • publish a new version of the service
