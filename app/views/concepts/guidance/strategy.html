Digital continuity Strategy

Our new online application for victims of sexual assault or abuse (non-complex) has entered Private Beta. A small number of users have agreed to participate in testing the application in a live environment.

Responsibilities

Below are the responsibilities for each department involved in the new online application service:

IT

  • Monitoring the new online application service for down time
  • Communicating any down time to the CSC Owner
  • Communicating any down time to Business Transformation
  • Investigating and cataloguing any issues with service delivery
  • Liaise with any 3 rd party providers to resolve any issues
  • Uploading and removing maintenance page
  • Communicating with CSC Owner when service has resumed
  • Communicating with Business Transformation when service has resumed

Customer Service Centre

  • AD Superusers/AD Lead to answer telephone calls from Private BETA users
  • Attempt to resolve any issues over the phone, for example check the URL to make sure it is correct
  • Raise any down time issues with Service Desk, completing issues log
  • Send communication to Business Transformation team, copying in the CSC Owner when system is down
  • Use scripts to cascade consistent message to users
  • Change IVR to inform users of down time, if necessary

Business Transformation

  • Cascade down time message to those stakeholders who are providing support with Private Beta
  • Signpost stakeholders to existing Justice Portal
  • Communicate with stakeholders when PB service has resumed

Process

A high-level version of the process has been mapped out below for each of the departments involved in CICA Online BETA testing:

IT

  • New Online Application for victims of Sexual assault or abuse goes down – this can be identified by system monitoring or by a phone call from an external user into the Customer Service Centre
  • E-mail CSC Owner, Business Transformation and copy in Head of Operations to advise that the New Online Application Service for victims of Sexual assault or abuse is down and follow up with face to face verbal update
  • Upload maintenance page
  • Investigate cause for downtime
  • Update CSC Owner/Business Transformation of likely down time duration – be as clear as possible around the number of hours/days it could take
  • Identify a solution – if necessary working with 3 rd party provider
  • Test and apply solution
  • Remove maintenance page
  • Notify CSC Owner/Business Transformation that system is working and provide reasons for the down time

Customer Service Centre

  • User advises there is an issue with the new Online Application Service for victims of Sexual assault or abuse
  • AD team attempt to resolve the issue over the phone. The advisor needs to identify if it is the new Online Application Service for victims of Sexual assault or abuse that is down or if it is user access issues, for example the wrong URL
  • If it cannot be resolved over the phone; the issue, user’s contact details and brief description of the issue, should be recorded on the Private Beta issues log
  • Inform Service Desk that the new Online Application Service for victims of Sexual assault or abuse is down. This can be done by the Assisted Digital Lead verbally and followed up with an IT ticket
  • Inform Business Transformation and the CSC Owner that it is down
  • CSC Owner to consider amending IVR to reflect down time issue. This is dependent on the length of time IT believe the system will be down, a quick fix (a couple of hours) will likely not need an IVR change
  • Use scripts to manage the user’s expectations regarding down time

Business Transformation

  • Receive communication from IT/CSC AD team
  • Send communication to all stakeholders who are supporting Private beta that service is down
  • Provide estimated timescale for down time
  • Offer alternative process – existing Justice Portal
  • Notify stakeholders when system is working again
