Guidance Eligibility check and triage

Questions to establish initial eligibility: CSC advisor

Ask the usual eligibility questions:

  • what is the incident you want to claim for? (is it a crime of violence?)
  • when did the incident happen? (was it within the last 2 years?)
  • where did the incident happen? (was it in Great Britain?)
  • did you report it to the Police? If so, when? (was there any delay in reporting?)
  • what were your injuries? (do the injuries qualify as per the Scheme?)
  • do you have any unspent criminal convictions? (would these stop them from qualifying?)

If they are eligible go on to First Triage for Private Beta.

If they are potentially ineligible

Continue with business as usual and:

  • monitor the new online application service for down time
  • communicate any down time to the CSC Owner
  • communicate any down time to Business Transformation
  • investigate and catalogue any service delivery issues
  • liaise with third-party providers to resolve any issues
  • upload and remove maintenance page
  • communicate with CSC Owner when service has resumed
  • communicate with Business Transformation when service has resumed

First triage for Private Beta: CSC advisor

View the triage process map.

Check if the user meets the criteria for Private Beta. You may have already established the answers to some of these questions during the eligibility check:

  • are you claiming as a victim of sexual assault or abuse?
  • do you have an email address?
  • are you a British citizen or EU national?
  • are you 18 or over?
  • are you applying for yourself or someone else?
If the user answers ‘no’ at any point in the triage

You do not need to ask further triage questions. Continue as usual by highlighting the benefits of current OAS:

  • it can be less traumatic
  • you can take as long as you want. No need to go through it all in one long phone call
  • if you don’t have all the information you can save as a draft and come back to it, take a break halfway through
  • you’ll get an email confirmation
  • you can describe things in your own words, not the words of an advisor

You should then direct the user to the current online application or take a telephone application.

If the user answers ‘yes’ to all the First Triage questions, move on to Second Triage for Private Beta.

Second triage for Private Beta: CSC advisor

The second triage will identify those who want to use our new application without support.

You should:

  • explain that we have a new online application service they can use
  • explain the benefits, for example it is simple and easy to use, has fewer questions, and you can do it in your own time
  • ask the user if they want to go online and do it themselves
If the user wants to go online themselves
  • note the user’s name and email address on the Private Beta spreadsheet
  • send them an email with the URL link to Private Beta
If the user wants to do it over the phone

Check that they have:

  • approximate date of crime
  • name of police force dealing with it
  • crime reference number
  • location where crime happened
If the user does not have the information needed for a telephone application

Tell the user:

  • to call back when they do
  • to select option 8 when they call
  • that they will be put straight through to an advisor who can take the new application
If the user has the information needed for a telephone application
  • advise them you will transfer them to an advisor who is trained to take the new application
  • transfer the call to one of the advisors on the assisted digital team:
  • Lorraine Mackenzie
  • Richard McGovern
  • Joseph Smith
  • Gregor Preston-Jones
  • Ross Little

Taking the new application: Assisted Digital team

  • use the Private Beta URL to take a telephone application
  • use the guidance to help support the user
  • note any issues or difficulties on the Private Beta feedback spreadsheet
  • include the case reference number once the application has been submitted
