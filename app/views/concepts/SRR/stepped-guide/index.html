Abolition of the ‘same roof’ rule

On XX May 2019 the government abolished the ‘same-roof’ rule, which had been part of the Criminal Injuries Compensation Scheme since 1 August 1964.

Under this rule victims could not receive compensation if their abuser was someone they were living with at the time of the abuse. The rule was changed on 1 October 1979 to apply only to adults who continued to live with their abuser.

What the change means

All victims abused by someone they lived with between 1 August 1964 and 1 October 1979 can now apply for compensation.

This includes victims who, because of the ‘same roof’ rule:

  • were previously refused compensation
  • did not apply for compensation

All applicants will still need to meet the remaining eligibility criteria.

If you have an ongoing application, you do not need to make an enquiry.

If you are not affected by the 'same roof' rule you should use our existing online application service.


The enquiry process

You must start an enquiry to give us enough information about you to assess if the ‘same roof’ rule affected you. There are three ways you can start an enquiry:

  • complete the online enquiry form
  • call us
  • email us

After we receive your enquiry

When we collect your information from the enquiry we will:

  • check our records for previous applications
  • check if the previous applications have enough information to make a new decision
  • ask you to provide more information if it is a new application, or your previous applications do not contain enough information
  • call you back, or keep you on the phone, to complete the application process
  • give you a named contact who will deal with you directly throughout the entire process
Other information we may need

When we call you back you may need to have the following information ready:

  • the date and location of the crime
  • the name of the police station where the crime was reported
  • your crime reference number
  • your GP’s name and address
  • your dentist’s name and address (if you had dental treatment because of your injuries)
  • details of any previous applications you have made to CICA
  • details of any unspent criminal convictions
  • proof of identity for you or anyone you are responsible for, such as a birth or marriage certificate, power of attorney or deed poll

Legal representatives

Our online enquiry service does not currently support legal representatives. You must apply by telephone.
