Apply for criminal injuries compensation

Use this service to apply for compensation if:

  • you were the victim of a sexual crime or abuse
  • you suffered physical or mental injuries because of a violent crime
  • a close relative died because of a violent crime
  • you saw a violent crime happen to a loved one, or were there immediately afterwards
  • you paid for the funeral of someone who died because of a violent crime

You must report the crime to the police. If it was not reported, we will not pay compensation.

The crime must have happened in England, Wales or Scotland. Northern Ireland has a different service.

Start now

Before you start

To complete the application we will ask you for:

  • an email address we can use to contact you
  • the date the crime happened
  • the police crime reference number
  • details of the where the crime happened

Compensation is paid by the UK government, not the offender. We can pay even if a court finds the offender not guilty or the police drop the charges.

Eligibility

Usually you must apply within 2 years of the crime happening. We may be able to make an exception if you:

  • were under 18 at the time
  • were advised to wait until a court case had ended
  • can show that you could not apply earlier

Other ways to apply

If you do not have internet access or an email address, or need help with the online form, you can contact us as follows:

  • inside UK 0300 003 3601, select option 8
  • outside UK +44 (0)203 684 2517

Help and support

Organisations that can give you free support and advice include:

  • Rape Crisis
  • Victim Support
  • Citizens Advice
