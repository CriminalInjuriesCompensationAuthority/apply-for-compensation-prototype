Apply for criminal injuries compensation

Use this service to apply for compensation if, because of a violent crime, physical or sexual assault or abuse:

  • you suffered physical injuries
  • you suffered mental injuries
  • a close relative died
  • you saw the crime happen to a loved one (or were there immediately afterwards)
  • you paid for the funeral of a person who died

Eligibility

Usually you must apply within 2 years of the crime happening. We may make an exception if:

  • you were a victim of childhood abuse
  • you were advised to wait until a criminal case had ended
  • you can show that you could not apply earlier, for example your mental or physical health stopped you

The crime must be reported to the police. If it is not reported, we cannot pay compensation.

The crime must have happened in England, Wales or Scotland. There is a different service for Northern Ireland.

We can pay compensation even if the court finds the offender not guilty.

Compensation is paid by the UK government, not by the offender.

What you'll need

To complete your application, you'll need:

  • an email address we can use to contact you
  • the date the crime happened
  • the crime reference number the police gave you when you reported the crime

Contact us if you do not have an email address or cannot use one for any reason.

Before you start

Help with your application

Some of the organisations that can give you free help or support include:

  • Rape crisis
  • Victim Support
  • Citizens Advice
  • Victims Information Service

You do not need to use a legal representative to help you with your application. Legal representatives typically charge between 20% to 30% of any compensation you may get.

Legal representatives

If you are a legal representative applying for compensation on behalf of a client, you should use the Apply for compensation as a legal representative service.
