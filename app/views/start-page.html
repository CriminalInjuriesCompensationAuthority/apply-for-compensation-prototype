Use this service to claim compensation if you were the victim of a sexual assault or abuse.

Compensation is paid by the government, not the assailant. It may be payable even if the assailant is not identified or convicted.

You need an email address to use this service. You will also be asked to provide the:

  • approximate date that the crime happened
  • crime reference number
  • location where the crime happened

Eligibility

You cannot get compensation if the crime has not been reported to the police.

The crime must have happened in England, Wales, Scotland or another relevant place.

There is a different service for Northern Ireland.

Usually you must apply within 2 years of when the crime happened. An exception may be made if one or both of the following applies:

  • you were under 18 at the time
  • you can show that you could not have applied earlier
