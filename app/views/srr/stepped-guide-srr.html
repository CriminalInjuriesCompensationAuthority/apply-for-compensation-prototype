Abolition of the ‘same roof’ rule

On XX May 2019 the government abolished the ‘same-roof’ rule.

The 'same roof' rule:

  • was part of the Criminal Injuries Compensation Scheme from 1 August 1964
  • stopped victims getting compensation if they lived with their abuser
  • changed on 1 October 1979 to apply only to adults who continued to live with their abuser

What the change means

Victims who lived with their abusers, and were abused between 1 August 1964 and 1 October 1979, can now apply for compensation.

This includes victims who, because of the ‘same roof’ rule:

  • had a compensation claim rejected
  • did not apply for compensation

All applications will still need to meet the remaining eligibility criteria.

If you have an ongoing application, you do not need to make an enquiry.

If you are not affected by the 'same roof' rule, you must use our online application service.

Making an enquiry

You must start an enquiry to give us enough information about you to assess if the ‘same roof’ rule affected you. There are four ways you can start an enquiry:

  • complete our online enquiry form
  • by phone
  • by email
  • by post

Making an enquiry

You can provide us with some basic information online to allow us to:

  • find any existing applications you may have, and call you back at a time convenient to you
  • or to arrange a callback to start a new application, at a time convenient to you.

Complete the online enquiry form

Other ways to enquire


Assessing your enquiry

When we receive your enquiry we will:

  • check our records for previous application
  • check if any previous applications have enough information to make a new decision
  • call you back, or keep you on the phone, to complete the application process
  • give you a single named contact who will deal with you throughout the entire process

Making an enquiry for someone else

The online form does not support enquiries from:

  • legal representatives
  • relatives or friends
  • people with legal responsibility for victims, for example, carers or guardians

If you are making an enquiry on behalf of someone else, you must contact us by phone.
