A disabling mental injury has a substantial adverse effect on your ability to carry out normal day to day activities. It does not include temporary mental anxiety, but may include:

  • impaired performance at work or school
  • negative effects on social relationships
  • negative impacts on your sex life and intimacy

Your disabling mental injury symptoms must have lasted for 6 weeks or more for us to consider paying compensation. We may also ask you to have your mental injuries assessed by a clinical psychologist.
