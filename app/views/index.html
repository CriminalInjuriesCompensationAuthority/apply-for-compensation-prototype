Apply for compensation for a criminal injury

{{releaseVersion | log }}

This prototype details iterations of the application process users will go through to apply for compensation if they have suffered an injury through being a victim of violent crime.

Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

Our MVP will cover users who have non-complex sexual assault applications.

Release 8MVP Sprint 3

Following user research we are iterating the design of the MVP service.

NEW FLOW

We are not using the stepped guide anymore and the sequence of the screens have been changed

Address look-up screen added, based on the video displayed here. Three possible situations:

  • the user has a UK address and once they submit their postcode they get list of possible addresses for it and select one (this is mocked)
  • the user has a UK address but doesn't know the postcode, they can enter it manually
  • the user doesn't have a UK address and is taken to a screen when they can enter the address in a single text. Warning: this might cause some IT issues as this format is not supported at the moment

Delay applying (over 2 years) is included now. You will be asked for a reason why you delayed applying if it's a:

  • single incident and the incident happened more than two years on the day of application
  • period of abuse and using the last day of the month of the end of the period of abuse, then it's more than 2 years from the day of application

For example:

  • the incident (single incident) was on the 1/01/2016 - if the applicant is applying on the 1/01/2018 it's fine, but if applying on 2/01/2018 or later, then after entering the incident date in the flow, they would see the screen "Why did you delay making your claim?"
  • the end of the period of abuse was on January 2016 - if the applicant is applying in January 2018 including on the 31/01/18 it's fine but if applying from the 1st of February then after entering the incident date in the flow, they would see the screen "Why did you delay making your claim?"

Delay reporting (more than 48h) is included now. You will be asked for a reason why you delayed reporting using the:

  • day of the incident if it's a single incident
  • last day of the month of the end of the period of abuse otherwise

For example:

  • the incident (single incident) was on the 1/01/2018 - if the applicant reported it on the 3/01/2018 it's fine, but if it was reported on 4/01/2018 or later, then after entering the date they reported the incident on in the flow, they would see the screen "Why was there a delay in reporting the crime to police?"
  • the end of the period of abuse was on January 2018 - if the applicant reported it on 02/02/2018 (so still within 48h after the end of the month = 31/01/2018) it's fine but if it was reported on the 3rd of February then after entering the date they reported the incident on in the flow, they would see the screen "Why was there a delay in reporting the crime to police?"

Release 0.6last updated 11/06/2018

This version is for usability testing on 12/06/2018 in Glasgow.

The focus of this journey is to see how simple we can make the application process by getting all but the simplest users out of the journey as early as possible.

Users that do not fall in to the simple group will be nudged in other directions. We have yet to define those other directions.

This journey focuses on users who:

  • are direct applicants
  • are over 18
  • are British citizens
  • do not have any criminal convictions
  • have applied for compensation from other sources
  • do not have ongoing medical treatment
  • have suffered personal injury

This release contains:

  • refinded content for most pages
  • removed question about obtaining a medical report
  • changes the stepped guide to bring it in line with the start page
